### 📝 Algorithms & Interview Prep

**Sites focused on data structures, algorithms, and technical interview preparation.** 🟢 Beginner → 🔴 Advanced

1. **[LeetCode](https://leetcode.com)** 🟢🔵🔴 - The most popular platform for coding interview preparation. It has thousands of problems ranging from easy to hard, with a strong focus on data structures and algorithms. Companies often use LeetCode problems in their technical interviews, making it essential for job seekers. *Tip: Focus on the "Top Interview 150" list for efficiency.*

2. **[HackerRank](https://www.hackerrank.com)** 🟢🔵 - A widely-used platform that offers coding challenges, competitions, and company-specific hiring contests. It covers multiple domains including algorithms, databases, and artificial intelligence. Many companies use HackerRank to screen candidates. *Tip: Try the "Interview Preparation Kit" track.*

3. **[GeeksforGeeks](https://www.geeksforgeeks.org)** 🟢🔵🔴 - A comprehensive computer science learning platform that combines tutorials with practice problems. It has detailed explanations, articles, and interview experiences from top tech companies. Great for learning concepts and seeing problem solutions. *Tip: Use the "Must Do Coding Questions" topic-wise list.*

4. **[HackerEarth](https://www.hackerearth.com)** 🟢🔵 - A platform that combines coding practice with hiring challenges. It offers problems across different difficulty levels and hosts hackathons. Many companies use it for recruitment and talent assessment. *Tip: Complete the "Code Monk" series for fundamentals.*

5. **[InterviewBit](https://www.interviewbit.com)** 🔵🔴 - An interview preparation platform with curated problems similar to those asked in top tech companies. It follows a structured learning path and tracks your progress. Great for systematic interview preparation. *Tip: Follow the structured path end-to-end for best results.*

6. **[NeetCode](https://neetcode.io)** 🔵 - A LeetCode-style platform specifically designed for interview preparation. It offers curated problem sets organized by company and difficulty. Popular among developers preparing for FAANG interviews. *Tip: Study by patterns rather than by company.*

7. **[AlgoMonster](https://algo.monster)** 🔵🔴 - An algorithms and data structures focused platform with a structured curriculum. It emphasizes pattern recognition and efficient problem-solving techniques. Popular for systematic algorithm practice. *Tip: Master the patterns before grinding random problems.*

8. **[CodeSignal](https://codesignal.com)** 🟢🔵 - A gamified coding practice platform with company-style assessments. Used by many companies for technical interviews. Good for practice and company-specific prep. *Tip: Take the "General Coding Assessment" to benchmark yourself.*

9. **[Interview Cake](https://www.interviewcake.com)** 🔵 - An interview prep platform with in-depth explanations and problems. Covers algorithms, data structures, and system design. Good for thorough interview preparation. *Tip: Read the full explanations even if you solve the problem.*

10. **[Codility](https://www.codility.com)** 🔵 - A platform used by companies for coding assessments. Offers training lessons and coding challenges. Good for practicing company-specific assessments. *Tip: Practice the "Lessons" section before attempting challenges.*

11. **[TryExponent](https://www.tryexponent.com)** 🔵🔴 - An interview preparation platform with practice problems and mock interviews. Focuses on helping you land jobs at top tech companies. *Tip: Use the mock interviews to simulate real pressure.*

12. **[Edabit](https://edabit.com)** 🟢 - An interactive platform offering bite-sized coding challenges for beginners. Problems are designed to teach programming concepts step by step. Perfect for those just starting their coding journey. *Tip: Great warm-up before diving into harder platforms.*

13. **[CodingBat](https://codingbat.com)** 🟢 - Simple practice problems in Java and Python, focused on basic logic and recursion. Created by Nick Partridge at UC Berkeley. Good for warming up with simple coding exercises. *Tip: Perfect for absolute beginners learning Java or Python.*

14. **[Daily Coding Problem](https://dailycodingproblem.com)** 🔵 - Delivers one coding problem to your inbox daily. Problems are similar to those asked in interviews. Great for consistent daily practice. *Tip: Subscribe and solve before looking at the solution.*

15. **[Learneroo](https://www.learneroo.com)** 🟢🔵 - Algorithms and coding practice platform. Offers structured learning paths for interview prep. Good for systematic practice. *Tip: Follow the learning paths for structured preparation.*

16. **[Codeintuition](https://codeintuition.com)** 🟢🔵 - A code learning platform for various programming topics. Offers lessons and practice for different skill levels. *Tip: Use alongside LeetCode for topic-specific drilling.*

17. **[CoderByte](https://coderbyte.com)** 🟢🔵 - Coding challenges and interview prep platform. Offers problems similar to interview questions. Good for interview practice. *Tip: Good for quick practice between other study sessions.*

18. **[Naukri Code360](https://code360.naukri.com)** 🟢🔵🔴 - Coding practice and interview prep popular in India. Offers problems, articles, and interview experiences. Good for placement preparation. *Tip: Popular for Indian placement preparation alongside LeetCode.*

19. **[Codemia](https://codemia.io)** 🔵🔴 - An active practice platform for system design interviews with AI-powered feedback. Covers system design, object-oriented design, DSA with visualizations, and peer mock interviews. Great for engineers targeting FAANG-level system design interviews. *Tip: Practice system design out loud like a real interview.*

### 🏆 Competitive Programming

**Platforms that host regular contests and competitions for algorithmic problem-solving.** 🟢 Beginner → 🔴 Advanced

1. **[CodeForces](https://codeforces.com)** 🔵🔴 - One of the most popular competitive programming platforms globally. It hosts regular contests featuring tough algorithmic problems. Known for its fast-paced contests and strong community of competitive programmers. Ideal for improving problem-solving speed. *Tip: Solve problems below your rating to build speed.*

2. **[CodeChef](https://www.codechef.com)** 🟢🔵 - A competitive programming platform that hosts monthly contests and provides a large problem library. It has a rating system and supports multiple programming languages. Good for those looking to compete in programming contests regularly. *Tip: Participate in Long Challenges for deep problem-solving.*

3. **[SPOJ](https://www.spoj.com)** 🔵🔴 - Sphere Online Judge is an online judge with a massive archive of problems. It has thousands of problems from various categories and supports many languages. Great for intensive practice with a large problem bank. *Tip: Search for classical problems sorted by difficulty.*

4. **[AtCoder](https://atcoder.jp)** 🟢🔵 - The most popular Japanese competitive programming platform. Hosts high-quality contests with algorithmic problems. Known for well-structured contests and problems. *Tip: Start with ABC (AtCoder Beginner Contest) problems.*

5. **[Kattis](https://open.kattis.com)** 🔵🔴 - A competitive programming problem archive used in many contests. Hosts problems from various programming competitions. Good for serious competitive programming practice. *Tip: Filter problems by difficulty using the Kattis difficulty score.*

6. **[UVa Online Judge](https://onlinejudge.org)** 🔵🔴 - The classic programming problem archive from the University of Valladolid. Contains over 4,000 problems from UVa and other contests. Great for serious problem-solving practice. *Tip: Use uHunt to track problems by topic and progress.*

7. **[DMOJ](https://dmoj.ca)** 🟢🔵 - An open-source programming judge that hosts many contests. Supports multiple languages and problem archives. Great for participating in contests and practice. *Tip: Great for Canadian contest preparation and practice.*

8. **[Timus Online Judge](https://acm.timus.ru)** 🔵🔴 - Russian programming contest problem archive. Hosts problems from Russian contests including Timus and ACM finals. Good for high-difficulty algorithmic practice. *Tip: Try the "Timus Top 50" for high-quality problems.*

9. **[Aizu Online Judge](https://onlinejudge.u-aizu.ac.jp/home)** 🟢🔵 - Japanese programming practice platform from Aizu University. Offers problems from Japanese programming contests. Good for competitive programming practice. *Tip: Use the "Course" section for structured learning.*

10. **[CSAcademy](https://csacademy.com/)** 🟢🔵 - Interactive computer science learning with a focus on algorithms. Offers courses and practice problems with an interactive editor. Good for learning algorithms visually. *Tip: Use the interactive visualizer for difficult algorithms.*

11. **[E-Olymp](https://e-olymp.com)** 🟢🔵 - An educational programming platform with problems and contests. Used by schools and universities for teaching programming. Good for students and educators. *Tip: Good for educational contest training.*

12. **[LightOJ](https://lightoj.com)** 🔵 - A programming problem collection with a wide variety of problems. Good for practice with diverse problem types. *Tip: Known for number theory problems — good for math practice.*

13. **[AlgoLeague](https://algoleague.com)** 🔵 - Algorithm competition platform that hosts regular contests. Good for participating in algorithm competitions. *Tip: Participate in live contests to test skills under time pressure.*

14. **[Toph](https://toph.co)** 🟢🔵 - Bangladeshi programming platform that hosts contests and practice problems. Supports multiple languages and has competitive programming contests. Good for regional contest participation. *Tip: Popular in Bangladesh — good for regional contest exposure.*

15. **[TLX TOKI](https://tlx.toki.id)** 🟢🔵 - Indonesian programming contest platform. Hosts national contests and practice problems. Popular in Indonesia for competitive programming. *Tip: Primary platform for Indonesian national programming contests.*

16. **[ACM HDU](https://acm.hdu.edu.cn)** 🔵🔴 - Chinese online judge from Hangzhou Dianzi University. Hosts many programming contests and has a large problem set. Popular in the competitive programming community. *Tip: Search problems by algorithm topic using the problem index.*

17. **[CodeAbbey](https://codeabbey.com)** 🟢 - Beginner-friendly programming problems with a community approach. Problems start easy and gradually increase in difficulty. Good starting point for new programmers. *Tip: Start here if you're new to programming — problems ramp gently.*

18. **[KEP](https://kep.uz)** 🟢🔵 - A programming platform based in Uzbekistan offering algorithmic problems and regular contests. Supports multiple languages and serves the Central Asian competitive programming community. Good for regional contest participation and practice. *Tip: Good for Central Asian competitive programming community access.*

19. **[Kilorova](https://kilorova.ro)** 🟢🔵 - A Romanian programming platform featuring algorithmic challenges and periodic contests. Includes problem sets from local competitions with multi-language support. Good for Eastern European competitive programmers. *Tip: Use for Romanian contest problems and local competition practice.*

20. **[Serious-OJ](https://serious-oj.com)** 🔵🔴 - An online judge featuring a curated collection of difficult algorithmic problems. Designed for experienced competitive programmers looking for challenges beyond typical problem sets. Good for advanced problem-solving and contest preparation. *Tip: Try the hardest problems only after solidifying basics elsewhere.*

21. **[Eldarverse](https://eldarverse.com)** 🟢🔵 - A programming platform offering algorithmic challenges and hosted contests. Features a clean interface with multi-language support and real-time submission feedback. Suitable for regular competitive programming practice. *Tip: Good supplementary practice alongside major platforms.*

22. **[andgein.ru](https://2026.andgein.ru/?lang=en)** 🔵 - A Russian programming competition platform that hosts a special New Year contest series alongside regular algorithmic challenges. Features a unique annual event structure with problems ranging from moderate to very difficult. Good for contest participation and seasonal practice. *Tip: Don't miss the annual New Year contest — it's unique.*

### 🌐 Full-Stack & Web Development

**Interactive courses and tutorials for building web development skills.** 🟢 Beginner → 🔴 Advanced

1. **[FreeCodeCamp](https://www.freecodecamp.org)** 🟢🔵 - A free full-stack curriculum that guides you through web development, data science, and more. It includes coding challenges and projects to build your portfolio. Completely free and self-paced. *Tip: Build the projects — they matter more than the challenges.*

2. **[W3Schools](https://www.w3schools.com)** 🟢 - The go-to resource for web development tutorials. Covers HTML, CSS, JavaScript, SQL, and many web technologies. Excellent for quick reference and learning web fundamentals. *Tip: Use as a reference, not a primary learning resource.*

3. **[Codecademy](https://www.codecademy.com)** 🟢🔵 - An interactive platform offering lessons in various programming languages. Lessons are hands-on with immediate feedback. Good for beginners wanting structured interactive learning. *Tip: Good for getting syntax down fast in a new language.*

4. **[The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com)** 🟢🔵 - A free full-stack web development curriculum. Teaches HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Ruby, and more through building projects. Excellent free alternative to coding bootcamps. *Tip: Commit to finishing the full path — it's worth it.*

5. **[Boot.dev](https://boot.dev)** 🟢🔵 - A backend developer learning path with courses in Python, Go, and more. Focuses on building real backend skills with projects. Good for those wanting to become backend developers. *Tip: Focus on the backend courses for depth in Python or Go.*

6. **[Newton School](https://www.newtonschool.com)** 🟢🔵 - A coding bootcamp and practice platform popular in India. Offers full-stack development courses with practice problems. Good for comprehensive coding education. *Tip: Popular for Indian placement prep with full-stack focus.*

7. **[SoloLearn](https://www.sololearn.com)** 🟢 - Mobile-friendly coding lessons in multiple languages. Designed for learning on the go with bite-sized content. Popular among mobile users and beginners. *Tip: Great for learning on mobile during commutes.*

8. **[Codedex](https://codedex.io)** 🟢 - An interactive programming lesson platform for beginners. Offers fun, bite-sized challenges in various languages. Great for learning programming basics in an engaging way. *Tip: Fun gamified entry point for complete beginners.*

9. **[CodeCrafters](https://codecrafters.io)** 🔵🔴 - A platform where you build popular developer tools like Git and Docker from scratch. Great for understanding how tools work under the hood. Excellent for deep learning and skill building. *Tip: Build each tool from scratch for deep systems understanding.*

### 🧩 Language & Niche Practice

**Platforms focused on specific languages, domains, or creative coding approaches.** 🟢 Beginner → 🔴 Advanced

1. **[CodeWars](https://www.codewars.com)** 🟢🔵🔴 - A gamified platform where you solve coding challenges called "katas" in various languages. It focuses on improving coding skills through repetition and mastery. Great for learning new languages and improving code efficiency. *Tip: Progress through kyu levels — each rank teaches new concepts.*

2. **[Advent of Code](https://adventofcode.com)** 🟢🔵🔴 - An annual December event with 25 daily programming challenges. Problems range from easy to very hard with a holiday theme. Fun community event for programmers of all skill levels. *Tip: Try to solve in a language you're learning.*

4. **[CodinGame](https://www.codingame.com)** 🟢🔵 - A unique platform that teaches programming through games and puzzles. You write code to control game characters and solve challenges. Fun way to practice coding in over 25 programming languages. *Tip: Great for learning new languages through puzzles.*

6. **[CodeGym](https://codegym.cc)** 🟢🔵 - A Java-focused online course with gamified learning. It takes you from zero to hero in Java programming. Great for learning Java through practical exercises. *Tip: Best for Java learners who want a structured gamified path.*

9. **[PyBites](https://pybitesplatform.com/)** 🟢🔵 - A Python coding practice platform with challenges and code bites. Offers real-world coding problems and promotes learning through practice. 10. **[4clojure](https://4clojure.oxal.org/)** 🔵 - Clojure programming challenges for learning functional programming. Teaches Clojure through interactive problems. Great for learning functional programming in Clojure. *Tip: Best for functional programming beginners in Clojure.*

12. **[PythonCode.club](https://pythoncode.club)** 🟢 - A Python-focused coding platform with problems organized by difficulty and topic. Covers fundamentals like data structures, algorithms, and standard library usage. Great for developers looking to strengthen their Python skills specifically. *Tip: Good supplementary Python practice alongside other platforms.*

13. **[Highload.fun](https://highload.fun)** 🔵🔴 - A specialized platform for high-performance and systems programming challenges. Problems focus on optimization, concurrency, memory management, and scalable design. Ideal for developers working on performance-critical applications. *Tip: Ideal for backend engineers exploring performance optimization.*

Covers topics like web exploitation, cryptography, reverse engineering, and system hardening. Good for learning practical cybersecurity through interactive problem-solving. *Tip: Try without hints first for maximum learning.*

15. **[SupeCoder](https://supecoder.dev)** 🟢 - An online coding platform with a growing library of algorithmic problems and regular contest events. Supports multiple programming languages with instant feedback on submissions. Suitable for general coding practice and skill building. *Tip: Good supplementary practice for contest preparation.*

16. **[CodeAny](https://codeany.me)** 🟢 - A lightweight coding platform designed for quick practice sessions with algorithmic problems. Features a clean, distraction-free editor and supports contest participation. Good for fitting in short, focused practice sessions. *Tip: Quick warm-up platform before longer study sessions.*

17. **[RepoVive](https://repovive.com)** 🟢 - A unique platform that lets you practice coding through real-world open source projects. Features curated issues and challenges from actual repositories across various languages. Great for building a portfolio and learning codebase navigation. *Tip: Great way to learn real-world code patterns.*

18. **[CodenQuest](https://codenquest.com)** 🟢 - A gamified coding platform where you solve problems to progress through quests and unlock achievements. Features a narrative-driven approach with progressively harder challenges. Makes coding practice feel like an adventure game. *Tip: Fun casual practice when you need a break from serious grinding.*

19. **[VIM Adventures](https://vim-adventures.com)** 🟢 - A game to learn VIM editor while playing. Teaches VIM commands through a fun, adventure-style game. Perfect for learning VIM efficiently. *Tip: Play through all levels to learn VIM navigation fluently.*

## 🛡️ CTF & Security Platforms

**CTF (Capture The Flag) sites are used for practicing cybersecurity skills and solving challenges.** 🟢 Beginner → 🔴 Advanced

1. **[TryHackMe](https://tryhackme.com)** 🟢🔵 - A beginner-friendly platform for learning cybersecurity through guided hacking labs. It offers step-by-step rooms and challenges for various skill levels. Perfect for those starting their cybersecurity journey. *Tip: Complete the "Pre Security" path before specializing.*

2. **[HackTheBox](https://www.hackthebox.eu)** 🔵🔴 - An advanced penetration testing practice platform. It offers challenging machines and CTF-style challenges. Popular among security professionals and enthusiasts. *Tip: Start with "Easy" machines before moving to complex ones.*

3. **[PicoCTF](https://picoctf.org)** 🟢 - A beginner-friendly CTF competition primarily for high school and college students. Problems range from easy to moderate difficulty. Great introduction to CTF competitions. *Tip: Perfect entry point for students new to CTF.*

4. **[OverTheWire](https://overthewire.org)** 🟢🔵 - A series of wargames for learning and practicing security concepts. Starts from very basics and progresses to advanced topics. Excellent for self-learning security. *Tip: Complete Bandit before attempting higher wargames.*

5. **[CTFLearn](https://ctflearn.com)** 🟢🔵 - A community-driven platform where users can practice CTF challenges. Offers problems in various categories. Good for beginners to advanced practitioners. *Tip: Sort by difficulty and progress gradually.*

6. **[CryptoHack](https://cryptohack.org)** 🟢🔵 - A platform for learning cryptography through challenges. Covers modern cryptography concepts and techniques. Great for learning and practicing crypto skills. *Tip: Work through the "Introduction" section first.*

7. **[W3Challs](https://w3challs.com)** 🔵 - A security challenges platform covering web, system, and network security. Offers challenges in multiple categories. Good for comprehensive security practice. *Tip: Good for testing web security skills in a structured way.*
